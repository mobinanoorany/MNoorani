Geo-SOM and Data Harmonization

Abstract Methods Visualization Conclusion

Abstract

This project focuses on harmonizing geo-located data in Jacksonville to explore the correlation between infrastructure, housing, and hazards for flood risk assessment. Using a Geo-SOM pipeline combined with hierarchical clustering, we created geographically coherent and feature-similar regions for data aggregation and visualization. These methods offer scalable, privacy-preserving solutions for decision-making and urban planning.

Methods

Unified Geo-SOM: Combines spatial and feature clustering in one model using KMeans for initialization and a two-phase BMU selection process. Hierarchical clustering refines the results for better interpretability.

Dual SOM Components: Uses separate SOMs for geographic and feature-based relationships. Results are integrated for refined clustering and better alignment with real-world boundaries.

Visualization

The left map shows cluster distributions based on geographic and feature similarities, while the right map aligns these clusters with real-world boundaries for actionable insights in flood risk analysis.

Conclusion

This study demonstrates the effectiveness of Geo-SOM and hierarchical clustering for generating regions that balance geographic coherence and feature similarity. The Unified Geo-SOM method outperformed the Dual SOM Components, providing scalable and privacy-aware solutions for urban planning and flood risk assessment. Future work could expand these methods to other regions and incorporate additional data layers.
