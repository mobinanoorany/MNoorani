Overview

This project leverages AI and GIS to assess student perceptions of flood risks on campuses. A web-based platform was developed to analyze, visualize, and understand these risks effectively.

Data Collection and Preprocessing

A dataset of georeferenced maps, satellite imagery, street view images, flood maps, density maps, and digital elevation models (DEMs) from 30 Gulf of Mexico university campuses was created. A total of 675,486 images were collected with an 80% coverage threshold for street view data.

Figure 1: Filtering campuses based on street view data availability.

Web Interface Design

The web interface integrates:

  • Macro-level data like satellite images.
  • Micro-level data like street view images.
  • Interactive clustering using Self-Organizing Maps (SOM).
  • Object detection via Inception-ResNet-v2 for risk analysis.

Figure 2: SOM visualization of campus environments.

Figure 3: Labeling interface per SOM cell.

Workshop and Data Analysis

205 participants provided insights into campus flood risks over a two-day workshop. Feedback included:

  • Identification of risky objects like trees and vehicles.
  • Perceptions of unsafe walking areas.
  • Suggestions for infrastructure improvements.

Figure 4: Word cloud of student concerns and Heatmap of keyword correlations.

Key Findings

Common risks identified include:

  • Falling trees during flooding events.
  • Damage to vehicles and residential properties.
  • Perception of street view images as non-representative of campus safety.

These findings highlight the need for targeted interventions to enhance campus safety and disaster preparedness.
